FAQ BLOCKS - 4 questions, each a standalone expandable card on the live site: 
 
1.  Will my KEAM rank be enough to get into B.Tech at KMCT? 
The honest answer: it depends on your rank, your preferred branch, and whether you're going through the 
government allotment or management quota. CSE and ECE fill faster. Civil and Mechanical have more 
seats. The fastest way to know? Call us with your rank - our admissions team will tell you in 5 minutes 
exactly which branches are open to you. We don't waste your time. 
 
2.  What is the total fee for B.Tech at KMCT - everything included? 
Government allotment fee and management quota fee are different. We publish both. Total fee means 
tuition + development fee + exam fee. Not just the annual tuition that looks lower on paper. Scroll to the 
Fee section below - it's all there. If you still have questions after reading it, call us. We will not quote you a 
number and then surprise you with additional charges. 
 
3.  Is the National Hospital College of Nursing, Pooladikunnu, INC recognised? Which hospital do 
students train in? 
Yes - INC recognised. KNC recognised. KUHS affiliated. Clinical training takes place at National Hospital, 
Kozhikode - a real hospital with real wards, real patients, real experience from your first year. Not a 
simulation lab. Not just theory classes. The hospital is named, the training is structured, and yes - after this 
degree, the Gulf, the UK, and Canada are real destinations. Our alumni are already there. 
 
4.  Is there a hostel? Is it safe? 
Yes. Separate hostels for men and women at Kallanthode. A dedicated women's hostel at Pooladikunnu 
for nursing students. Resident wardens on-site - not visiting, resident. Meals included. Transport available. 
Parents of nursing students: the hostel is the first thing we show you when you visit, because we know it is 
the first thing you need to see. See full hostel details below.

07 Admissions - How It Actually Works 
Process clarity. Removes fear. Converts hesitation into enquiry. 
 
Getting Into KMCT Is Simpler Than the Entrance Process . 
Admissions 2026–27: KEAM, Management Quota, NRI - made simple 
Kerala's college admission process can feel like a maze. KEAM, allotments, CEE rounds, 
management quota - it is a lot. Here is exactly what happens when you want to join KMCT, step by 
step. If you get lost anywhere in this, one phone call sorts it out. 
 
1 
Check Your Eligibility - It Takes 2 Minutes 
B.Tech: KEAM rank (government quota) or Plus Two Physics, Chemistry, Maths (management 
quota). B.Sc Nursing / GNM: Plus Two with Physics, Chemistry, Biology — government quota 
through Kerala CEE nursing entrance, management quota directly. B.Arch: NATA score required. 
B.Pharm: KEAM rank or management quota with PCM or PCB. BBA / MBA / MCA: Plus Two for 
BBA, any degree for MBA. B.Ed / D.Ed: Any degree with relevant subject. See full eligibility → 
 
2 
Call Us or Fill the Enquiry Form - We Respond Within Hours 
Tell our admissions team: your rank or marks, your preferred programme, your district. We will tell 
you in the same conversation: which branches are available to you, whether government allotment 
or management quota applies to your situation, what the exact fee will be, and what seats are left. 
No runaround. No vague answers. One call. 
 
3 
Visit Campus - Most Families Decide the Same Day 
Come and see. Engineering labs. Architecture studio. Pharmacy labs. Nursing college and 
National Hospital, Kozhikode where clinical training happens. Hostel facilities — men's at 
Kallanthode, women's at both campuses. Most families who visit once, confirm their seat before 
they leave. 
 
4 
Confirm Your Seat - Before Someone Else Does 
Management quota seats at KMCT fill quickly — especially CSE, ECE, and B.Sc Nursing. If you 
are interested, do not wait for the next allotment round to check. Call us now and we will tell you 
what is available today. 
 
KEAM Quota Admissions

30 Years of Building Careers in North Kerala. 
 
The Story - Left Column 
Dr. K. Moidu founded the Kunhitharuvai Memorial Charitable Trust in 1994 with that question. The first 
answer was a pharmacy college. Then an engineering college. Then engineering for women. Then 
nursing, architecture, polytechnic, business management, teacher education. 
Nine institutions built over three decades - not because of a business plan, but because each one 
was an answer to a question Kerala's students were asking. 
Today, the group is led by Managing Trustee Dr. KM Mehboob, a neurologist in Kozhikode, who asks 
the same question every admissions season: are the students who came to us last year better off 
because they did? 
The answer - built across 30 years and thousands of graduates - is yes.
