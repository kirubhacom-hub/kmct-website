How the KMCT Group Was Built - Nine Institutions, Thirty Years, Two Campuses.

The Story
The Kunhitharuvai Memorial Charitable Trust was founded in Kozhikode in 1994 by Dr. K. Moidu - a physician who recognised that students from North Kerala had limited access to professional education without leaving the state. The first institution was a pharmacy college.

Engineering came next, in 2001, at the Kallanthode campus 25 kilometres from Kozhikode city. Then a polytechnic, because the diploma route deserved the same quality of institution that engineering students received. Then an engineering college specifically for women, because an all-women technical campus serves a different need - and serves it well.

The nursing college at Pooladikunnu was a response to a specific gap: government nursing seats in Kerala are heavily oversubscribed, clinical training quality varies, and families from North Kerala were sending daughters to institutions far from home with little visibility into what the training actually looked like. The National Hospital College of Nursing was built attached to National Hospital, Kozhikode - so that students train in a real hospital from day one.

Architecture, business management, teacher education and teacher training followed over the years - each one added because the demand existed and no institution of adequate quality was meeting it locally. In 2015, KTU affiliation brought all engineering programmes under APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University - the same university that governs government engineering colleges in Kerala.

Thirty years. Nine colleges. The founding rationale has not changed. Today, under Managing Trustee Dr. KM Mehboob, the KMCT group runs nine institutions across two campuses. The founding question has not changed. Every admissions season, it is still being asked - and the answer is still being built.

Campus Life at KMCT - Hostel, Labs, Transport and Facilities.

Laboratories and Workshop Facilities
Engineering labs equipped to AICTE standards across all eight B. Tech departments. Architecture studio. Pharmacy labs to PCI specifications. Computer labs. Electronics workshop. Nursing students use clinical facilities at National Hospital, Kozhikode - not a campus simulation lab.

Hostel Accommodation
Separate hostels for men and women at Kallanthode. Resident wardens-on-site, not visiting. Meals included. Wi-Fi. Generator backup. 24-hour security.
Pooladikunnu: dedicated women's hostel for nursing students. Resident warden. Close proximity to National Hospital where clinical training is conducted.

Student Events - Karma, Metanoia and Technical Clubs
Karma and Metanoia - the flagship engineering and arts fests. Student technical clubs, IEEE student branch, IEDC. Intercollegiate competitions. Cultural events.

Transport
College bus routes from Kozhikode, Malappuram, Kannur and Wayanad. Day scholars from within the region commute daily. Route details and stop information on the Contact page. For hostel students: transport is available for city visits on weekends. Schedules confirmed each semester.
